Conclusions

  • On static societies, a presentation protocol is enough to know one another because the ‘other’ is considered in their design. They has the same language and ontology.

  • Role identification by behaviour observation is a good approach for open societies.

    • In these societies, agents can migrate to another society and these migrating agents may know something about the others there.
    • Moreover, they couldn’t know anything about them a priori (what language/protocol do they use?, for example)
    • They can only observe others.
